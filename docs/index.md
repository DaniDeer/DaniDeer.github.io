# Software Engineering Lessons, Patterns, and Practical Guidance from the Trenches of a Legacy Manufacturer

> by Daniel Hirsch

## Welcome

Welcome to my personal blog, where I share insights, lessons, and practical guidance from my journey as a software engineer in the manufacturing domain here in Germany. This blog is a collection of my experiences, challenges, and solutions that I have encountered while working with legacy systems and modern software engineering practices.

I started as a PLC programmer on the shopfloor working on mechatronic systems of one of the biggest automotive manufactuerers here in Germany. Over the years, I find my journey to the software engineering domain working on I4.0 transformation strategy and solutions.

As a young software engineer, I challenged many beliefs and practices of people working in this legacy environment for years and often (intentionally) ruffled some feathers in the process. I am knowning of speaking up what I think, and I am grateful to work in an environment where this is appreciated and encouraged - so take this with a grain of salt.

In my blog you will read about technologies like `Docker`, `Kubernetes`, `Terraform` and `CI/CD pipelines`, programming languages like `Go` and `Python` and how `Haskell` and `Functional Programming` influence my thinking and approach to software engineering and architecture. How we find ways to use `AI` to solve problems in the manufacturing domain and not only to write chatbots or use `AI` only for code generation - instead using it as an additional tool in our engineering toolbelt to enhance our solutions.

Welcome to my blog, and I hope you find it insightful and helpful in your own software engineering journey! Happy reading!
